A collection of thinking frameworks that anyone can use to reason more clearly, make better decisions, and understand the world.

Concepts drawn from Farnam Street's Mental Model library. Website design inspired by lawsofux.com.

First Principles

Break a problem down to what you know is actually true, then build your answer up from there.

Inversion

Instead of planning for success, ask what would make this fail and work backward from that.

Second-Order Thinking

Before deciding, ask "and then what?" Most problems come from only thinking one step ahead.

Circle of Competence

Know where your knowledge actually ends. Operating just past that line is where most bad decisions happen.

Occam's Razor

When you have multiple explanations for something, start with the simplest one that fits the facts.

Map vs. Territory

The way you see a situation is not the situation itself. Your mental picture is always an incomplete version of reality.
