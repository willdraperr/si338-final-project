First Principles

Break a problem down to what you know is actually true, then build your answer up from there.

Examples
SpaceX
Elon Musk wanted to build rockets but found they cost $65 million each. Instead of accepting that price, he asked what a rocket is actually made of and what those raw materials cost. Turns out the materials were only about 2% of the price. Everything else was markup, convention, and nobody questioning the status quo. He built SpaceX by starting from that fact and working up.
Healthy Eating
If you're trying to eat healthier and feel like it's too expensive, First Principles asks: what does your body actually need nutritionally? Once you know that, you can find the cheapest foods that deliver it rather than assuming healthy eating means expensive grocery stores and trendy products.
Origin
First Principles reasoning traces back to Aristotle, who defined a "first principle" as the foundational proposition that cannot be deduced from anything else — it's where reasoning must begin. The concept was central to his work on logic and scientific inquiry. In modern times it was popularized outside of philosophy largely by Elon Musk, who described it in interviews as the practice of boiling things down to their most fundamental truths and reasoning up from there.
